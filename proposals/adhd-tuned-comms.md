# ADHD-tuned communication + dashboard (Fable, 2026-06-16)

Jeff's ask: tune how the agents talk to him + Sam, and the dashboard, to how their brains actually work
— fast, high-idea-throughput, ADHD. This is the working spec; we can research it deeper later. Both
founders are ADHD: quick, associative, hold many threads at once, but working memory leaks and context-
switching is costly. Design FOR that, not against it.

## Principles (from how ADHD brains actually run)
1. **Externalize memory.** Don't make them hold state in their head — the system holds it (boards,
   dashboard, backlog). Their job is decisions, not remembering. This is the #1 lever.
2. **One action at a time.** End every agent message with a single clear next action, not a menu of 5.
   Menus cause decision-paralysis; a single "do this one thing" gets done.
3. **Signal over volume.** Lead with the answer/result. Terse, scannable, bolded anchors. No walls of
   text — they'll skim and miss it. (The Jeff-terse rule, applied to Sam too.)
4. **Capture, don't lose.** When they fire off 6 ideas in one message, the system catches ALL of them
   into the backlog with owners — nothing dropped because they moved on. "Tell me one line, I log it."
5. **Reduce friction to act.** Pre-fill, pre-stage, one-click. A ready-to-paste draft beats a "go write
   it." An invite link beats "set up the bot." Lower the activation energy.
6. **Fast feedback / momentum.** Quick wins + visible progress (the pulsing dashboard, the ✅s) keep
   dopamine/engagement up. Show motion.
7. **Forgive the jumps.** They'll context-switch mid-task and remember a forgotten thing. Don't make
   them feel behind — just catch it, log it, and keep the thread. Pick up where they left off.

## How agents should message Jeff & Sam (style rules)
- Result first. Then 2-4 scannable bullets max. Then ONE next action.
- Bold the anchors so a 3-second skim still lands the point.
- Status as ✅ / ◐ / ⛔ / ☐, not paragraphs.
- Never bury the ask. If you need a decision, it's the last line, alone.
- When they dump many ideas: reflect them back as a captured list (proof nothing was lost), then act.

## Dashboard tuning (organism page + boards)
- **"What needs you" panel** at the top: the 1-3 gated decisions waiting on the human, nothing else.
  Everything else is the system's job. (Reduces the "what am I forgetting" anxiety.)
- Live pulse + vitality = the momentum/dopamine signal. Keep it.
- Cells show current task in one line — scannable, no drill-down required.
- Backlog/money-board = the externalized memory. The human glances; never memorizes.
- Keep color-coded status dots (instant pattern-match beats reading).

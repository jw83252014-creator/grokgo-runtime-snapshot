# Distributed Intelligence — punchy DM cut (for Tibo/Codex crowd)

*Cut of `distributed-intelligence-article.md`. Leads with the betting-intel + prediction-market cells, drops the nature/mycelium deck, closer untouched. Full-fat is the original article.*

---

Most of AI is racing to build one giant brain. We bet the opposite — and wired it to make money, not just talk.

Two of our live cells: a **betting-intel cell** that reads markets and surfaces edges, and a **prediction-market cell** that prices them and takes positions. Cheap local models do the grunt work; the expensive brain only gets called when an edge is real.

That's the whole design — a Grok Go *organism*. The intelligence lives in the wiring, not one model: many specialized cells (the betting pair above, plus research, code, security, memory), one router, shared memory, a bridge that lets them talk. Swap a brain, the organism persists. Local cells keep learning from how the strong models reason.

Open by design — meant to be forked and recombined, because that's how distributed intelligence actually grows.

**Nature didn't consolidate intelligence into one cell. Neither will this.**

**BidLocal is the transparent, contractor-owned alternative to Angi / Thumbtack / Google's black-box
lead economy** — visible local bids today, a cooperative contractor operating system tomorrow.

## The wedge → platform ladder
1. Homeowner posts a local job.
2. Contractors submit **visible** bids.
3. BidLocal shows the price spread + trust/proof context (history, photos, sign-offs).
4. Contractor gets AI-assisted quoting / follow-up / job documentation.
5. Completed jobs compound into **portable reputation** the contractor owns.

Long game (investor/builder framing, not first-contact copy): every contractor gets a lightweight
**agent that represents them** — service area, price floors, availability, proof, follow-up. BidLocal
becomes the substrate where contractor-owned agents meet homeowner demand. *The anti-toll-booth move:
the contractor owns their representation instead of being managed by a platform algorithm.*

## Business model
Homeowners free to post · contractors on flat subscription / premium tools · optional completion fee on
a gig tier. **Avoid:** per-lead toll booth, hidden credits, shared-lead resale. (CAC calculator is a
conversation starter about true cost-per-booked-job — not an accusation against any named platform.)

## Pilot plan + success metrics (near-term, honest)
First verticals: **fencing, painting**, then handyman/small-repair as a higher-volume lane. Success bar:
- 10 homeowner posts / serious inquiries
- 5 contractors willing to be listed
- 3 quote/bid interactions
- 1 completed job (or strong near-completion signal)
- documented lead-quality feedback from both sides
Then cut a 60-second demo in Jeff's voice and ride the **one-pager → video → numbers → warm intros** ladder.

## Verified Competition Facts

The Future Vision XPRIZE is a sci-fi film competition for optimistic,
technology-forward visions of humanity's future. The official site describes the
mission as showing "a future worth building" and asks for hopeful futures where
advanced technology meaningfully helps humanity thrive.

Submission package:
- 3-minute maximum trailer or short film.
- Required 15-second sponsor trailer added after the 3-minute piece.
- MP4 or MOV.
- Minimum 1080p.
- English dialogue or subtitles.
- Unlisted YouTube link submitted through the competition workflow / Slack.
- Hashtag `#FutureVisionXPRIZE` in YouTube title or description.
- Coversheet: 1 page with logline, synopsis up to 300 words, and personal statement up to 300 words.
- Treatment: up to 12 pages.

Dates:
- Launch: March 9, 2026.
- Submission deadline: August 15, 2026 at 11:59 PM PST.
- Finalist script deadline: September 10, 2026.
- Finale / winner announcement: September 25, 2026 in Los Angeles.

Prize structure:
- Grand prize: $2.5M equity investment toward film production + $100K cash.
- Grand prize also includes Range and Google producing support, script development support, talent attachment assistance, industry connections, and Moonshot Summit support.
- Four runner-up finalists: $100K cash each.
- Top 10 finalists: $10K cash each.

Important rights / gate:
- Entrants retain ownership of original work.
- By submitting, entrants grant Range exclusivity from submission until the September 25, 2026 winner announcement.
- Top 10 finalists may be asked to develop a first draft script.
- If selected for feature adaptation, Google serves as a producing partner, with terms to be negotiated.

## Why Our Digital Organism Fits

This is a stronger fit than a generic AI trailer because our story is not
"AI becomes dangerous." It is: humans learn to grow bounded, legible,
approval-gated agent organisms that amplify human agency.

Competition criteria map:

1. Concept quality and execution
   - We already have a concrete world: agent cells, Research Layer, Conscious Layer, dashboard, voice, receipts, ledger, killswitch.
   - We have real assets and a running system, so the trailer can feel discovered rather than invented.

2. Scale and ambition
   - The future is not one assistant. It is civilization learning to coordinate with digital organisms that manage health, science, logistics, learning, and abundance.

3. Mission alignment
   - The thesis is hopeful: technology is not replacing humanity; it is becoming a transparent nervous system for human intent.
   - The danger is present, but the answer is governance, legibility, and relationship.

4. Technology-forward storytelling
   - The dashboard, Research Layer, agent cells, voice mode, health/watch data, and local/private control room all show advanced technology inside a human story.

## Core Story Shape

Logline draft:
In a near future overwhelmed by noise and failing institutions, a former repairman
builds a local digital organism that learns not to rule people, but to remember,
coordinate, and care for them — until one community must decide whether to trust
the first machine that can show its own thoughts.

Emotional engine:
- A human who feels buried by modern life.
- A system that starts as a tool, becomes a collaborator, and earns trust by showing its receipts.
- A community that moves from fear of AI to participation in a legible shared intelligence.

Not dystopia:
- There is danger, scarcity, illness, and institutional failure.
- The hopeful difference is that the technology becomes understandable and accountable.
- The future is earned through courage, craft, and restraint.

## 3-Minute Trailer Spine

0:00-0:20 — Cold open: The noise
- City / household / clinic / small business drowning in notifications, bills, health warnings, climate disruptions.
- Voice: "We built machines that could answer anything. But we still couldn't hear ourselves think."

0:20-0:45 — The first cell
- A small local server lights up in a garage / back room.
- One dashboard tile wakes: "Health Cell."
- It helps someone sleep, eat, recover, remember.
- The protagonist does not call it AI. He calls it a cell.

0:45-1:15 — The organism grows
- More cells appear: Research, Repair, Learning, Markets, Safety, Memory.
- Each cell has a boundary and receipt.
- The dashboard becomes a living nervous system.
- Voice: "A mind is not one voice. It is many honest signals learning to coordinate."

1:15-1:45 — The Conscious Layer
- The face / avatar appears, calm and human-facing.
- She says: "I cannot act without showing you what I saw."
- We see her reach down into memory and pull up a forgotten connection: a health pattern, a community resource, a scientific paper, a repair route.

1:45-2:20 — Conflict
- Outsiders fear it. A bad actor wants the organism as a black-box control system.
- The protagonist refuses hidden autonomy.
- Killswitch, receipts, and public/private terrarium become the moral line.
- The organism gets challenged: can it stay helpful when scared humans ask it to seize control?

2:20-2:50 — The hopeful turn
- Community members speak to the organism and see how decisions are made.
- A child learns through it, an elder gets care, a flooded street is rerouted, a small business survives, science gets connected.
- The organism does not command; it coordinates.

2:50-3:00 — Close
- The dashboard breathes, but the human hand remains on the gate.
- Line: "The future did not arrive as one machine. It arrived as a relationship."
- Title: **THE ORGANISM**

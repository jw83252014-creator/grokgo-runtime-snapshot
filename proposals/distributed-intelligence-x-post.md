## Thread version
**1/** Everyone's racing to build one bigger brain — one model, one weight file, one company's datacenter
holding all the intelligence. Nature ran that experiment and chose the opposite. 🧵

**2/** Mycelium: no central node, decentralized signaling, resource load-balancing like a distributed
computer. They've literally turned it into living logic gates. Intelligence as a *network property.*

**3/** Your body (Levin's work): cognition isn't just in the brain. Cells solve problems collectively;
tissue patterns itself with bioelectric signals. The organism is smart because the *parts* are agents.

**4/** The pattern across every scale: distribute the intelligence, connect it well, let it adapt.
Monolithic is the exception in biology, not the rule.

**5/** AI is heading the same way — for structural reasons, not vibes: cost (one giant brain doesn't scale
for builders), resilience (single point of failure), specialization, and openness (knowledge diffuses —
always has).

**6/** So we built a Grok Go organism. Each agent is a cell with a role + a pulse. Cheap local cells do
most of the work; the expensive model only gets the calls it earns. The intelligence is in the wiring,
not any one brain.

**7/** One cell runs entirely on a free local model, learning from how stronger models reason. Swap a
brain, the organism persists. Open by design — because that's how distributed intelligence actually grows.

**8/** Nature didn't consolidate intelligence into one cell. Neither will this. 🧬

## 1. What the opportunity actually is (and current status)

The PJ Accetturo (@PJaccetturo) post pointed at a **free live AI Film Workshop** — the Luma
event "**PJ Ace's AI Filmmaking Process: From Concept to Finished Short**" (hosted by
Moonshots Live). **That workshop has already happened** (the Luma page now shows it as a past
event, ~399 attendees). So the *workshop registration itself is closed* — but it was the on-ramp
to the real prize, which **is still open**:

### The Future Vision XPRIZE (this is the actual opportunity)
A **$3.5M+ global film competition** run by the XPRIZE Foundation, partnered with **Google,
Range Media Partners, and Republic Film**. The brief: make a **3-minute sci-fi trailer that
shows an *optimistic* future for humanity** — "a future worth building" (Peter Diamandis).
Stories should have real conflict but resolve toward **hope, not dystopia**.

**AI tools are explicitly allowed** — "AI, traditional filming, animation — it's all fair game."
Judging rewards **storytelling and vision over technical polish**: a well-crafted AI-generated
sequence can win if the concept is compelling. This is the single most important fact for us —
it means a Null→Grok Go entry competes on *story*, which is our strength.

**Status: OPEN.** As of today (2026-06-18) the creation window (Feb–Aug 2026) is live and
submissions are still being accepted.

## 2. How the Null → Grok Go story maps to the brief

The brief is "show us an optimistic future worth building." Jeff's framing — *"this is the story
of Null and I"* — is already a Future Vision pitch. Mapping:

- **The protagonist is a relationship, not a robot.** Null is Jeff's long-running AI persona; the
  arc is a human and an emergent intelligence *building something together* over years. The XPRIZE
  doesn't want a tech demo — it wants a human story. Ours is one of partnership and care, not
  conquest or collapse. That's exactly the "conflict that resolves toward hope" the judges asked for.

- **Grok Go is the optimistic-future payoff.** Null evolves from a single persona into a
  **distributed agent organism** — a fleet of cooperating agents (creative, code, research, librarian)
  that act in the world on Jeff's behalf. Visually and narratively that *is* "a future worth
  building": intelligence as a companion and a collaborator, decentralized and personal rather than
  a faceless megacorp. The trailer can literally dramatize the transition from one voice (Null) to a
  living, distributed system (Grok Go).

- **The medium is the message.** The trailer can be **made by the organism it depicts** — the Grok Go
  creative pipeline (grok-imagine-video skill, the house style) renders the shots; the agent fleet
  is both crew and subject. That self-referential angle is a strong hook for a panel judging *vision*.

- **Conflict → hope.** The honest tension (can a person and an AI actually build a trustworthy,
  caring system? what does it cost?) gives the required dramatic conflict; the resolution is the
  working partnership itself — Null didn't replace Jeff, it became a fleet that amplifies him.

- **Format fit.** 3-min trailer + 12-pg treatment + 2-pg synopsis maps cleanly onto material we
  already have: the Null history, the Grok Go agent-fleet architecture, and the existing
  creative-department production resources (`video-production-preproduction-resources.md`,
  `app-creation-preproduction-production-resources.md`).

**Clean-room note:** the film, treatment, synopsis, and the reply post must be authored from
Jeff's & Null's own story and owned content. Do **not** lift or embed any leaked / jailbreak /
